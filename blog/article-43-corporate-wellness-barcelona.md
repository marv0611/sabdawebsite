None of that works. Here's what does — and why doing it in Barcelona, in the right space, changes the equation entirely.

---

## Why most corporate wellness fails

It fails because it's bolted on. A wellness session squeezed between a quarterly review and a Slack notification is not wellness — it's another to-do. The environment matters as much as the activity. A conference room with the chairs pushed back is still a conference room. Your team's nervous system knows it.

For wellness to actually shift something in a team, three things need to happen: the environment needs to be radically different from work, the activity needs to create genuine vulnerability (not forced "sharing"), and the experience needs to be shared — not individual.

## What actually works

### Immersive group breathwork
Breathwork in a 360° projection room with spatial audio creates a group experience that office settings can't produce. Fifty people breathing together, eyes closed, surrounded by shifting light and sound — the professional masks drop. People who've worked together for years say things they've never said. The shared vulnerability creates connection that no icebreaker can manufacture.

### Sound healing
Lying down together in silence while frequencies wash over the group is the opposite of every corporate interaction. No agenda, no action items, no deck. Just shared stillness. Teams report that the post-session openness carries into the next day's meetings.

### Breathwork + ice bath
For teams that want something intense: guided breathwork inside the immersive room followed by cold water immersion. The ice bath is where it happens — the combination of breath control and extreme cold cuts through every professional facade. Teams that do this together talk about it for months.
